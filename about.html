Mind-Body-Computer

It was Descartes that first formulated the mind-body problem, suggesting that the mind and body are separate entities (dualism). As humans progress, as technology advances, we broaden our horizons formulating new questions and new problems.

Today the problem has transformed to not just mind-body but also mind-body-computer, the human-computer interaction. Ideally the gap between humans and computers will be seamlessly bound together enhancing the user experience in the process.

About Me

As a problem-solver I sought out on this enriching journey. I began my journey at UC San Diego studying in the field of Cognitive Science and specializing in Human-Computer Interaction. With my minor in History I have been given the additional tools to look back and learn from the past to then set a new and improved path into the future. A future filled with simple, easy-to-use, and enjoyable interactions.
